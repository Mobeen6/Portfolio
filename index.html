Economics Research · Presented at WB SAESM 2025

When the Waters Recede: The Lasting Impact of Pakistan's 2010 Floods on Learning Outcomes

The 2010 floods displaced ~20 million people and inflicted US$10 billion in economic losses. This paper examines whether children of school-going age at the time show systematically lower literacy outcomes a decade later.

Novel Flood Severity Index (FSI)

Built in QGIS using NASA MODIS satellite imagery and NDMA damage reports — a continuous district-level exposure measure:

FSI = 0.45 × (% Area Inundated) + 0.40 × (% Population Affected) + 0.15 × (Days Inundated)

Ranges from near-zero in unaffected districts to above 55 in the most severely hit, concentrated along the Indus River corridor in southern Punjab and northern Sindh.

Methodology

Short-run: Difference-in-Differences using PSLM 2008–09 (baseline) and 2010–11 (post-flood), across 138,687 observations.

Long-run: Age-at-exposure framework comparing literacy in 2019–20 across cohorts aged 5–16 (treated) vs. those too young or too old at the time of the floods.

Key Findings

−0.05 pp

Literacy decline per unit FSI increase in the short run

0.057 SD

Long-run literacy reduction per 1 SD in FSI, a decade later

−13.9 pp

Female literacy penalty — girls bear disproportionately larger losses

1.5×

Rural amplification relative to urban children with equal flood exposure

Policy Implications

Argues for a shift from reactive relief to anticipatory resilience: conditional cash transfers targeting girls, climate-proofed school infrastructure, accelerated learning programmes, and community-based continuity models.

Exploratory Data Analysis · Python · Kaggle

AeroFit Treadmill Customer Profiling

End-to-end EDA on 180 AeroFit customer records across three treadmill models (KP281, KP481, KP781). Covers univariate, bivariate, multivariate analysis, outlier detection, and conditional probabilities — culminating in actionable marketing recommendations.

Key Customer Profile

Typical buyer: young adult (24–33), college-educated, moderate fitness (level 3–4), income $44K–$58K. Most use treadmills 3–4× per week and run 66–114 miles/month. Males and partnered individuals dominate purchases.

Product Segmentation

KP281 (44% of sales): Entry-level, popular among lower-income, lower-fitness buyers.
KP481 (33%): Mid-range, balanced demographics.
KP781 (22%): Premium — high-income ($83K–$104K), 93.5% rate fitness level 5, run 150+ miles/week.

Strongest Correlations

Fitness ↔ Miles: 0.79  ·  Usage ↔ Miles: 0.76  ·  Usage ↔ Fitness: 0.67  ·  Education ↔ Income: 0.63

Recommendations

Market KP781 as a high-performance athlete product. Introduce installment plans for high-usage lower-income buyers. Target female KP781 buyers via fitness influencer campaigns. Create couple/family fitness packages — 59% of buyers are partnered.

Fintech Strategy Consulting

Bank Alfalah: The A.M.F.A.B. Solution

A consulting deck proposing a five-point fintech innovation roadmap for Bank Alfalah — Pakistan's fourth-largest bank — against a backdrop of 8% quarterly mobile banking growth and an $8B SME lending gap.

The A.M.F.A.B. Framework

A — Alfa App Optimization: UI overhaul with 24/7 virtual assistant, "Expected Amount" display, Tap-to-Report fraud, NFC tap-to-pay. Projected: 20% increase in high-value transactions, 25% fraud complaint reduction.

M — Modernize Branches: Smart Kiosks and parallel banking model across 50 flagship locations. 65% reduction in processing times, 40% teller load reduction. Phased rollout over 1–5 years.

F — Financial Advisory (Shariah): Islamic Banking HelpDesk with advisory on Sukuks and Takaful. First-mover advantage; projected 5–10% of total revenue. Targets Pakistan Banking Vision 2028.

A — AI Risk Assessment: AI-based loan eligibility replacing collateral. Real-time fraud detection. Projected: 25% more loan approvals = PKR 5B in new loans.

B — Blockchain KYC: Onboarding from days to minutes. Smart contract cross-border transactions. Capturing 1% of Pakistan's $31B remittance market = PKR 5B in volume.

Financial Projections

Net Advances: PKR 856B → PKR 941B  ·  PAT: PKR 27.5B → PKR 31.5–33B  ·  Cost-to-Income: 42.5% → 35–40%

Competitive Position

Under this model, Bank Alfalah becomes the first Pakistani bank with full AI-powered fraud prevention, complete smart digital branches, and blockchain KYC — leapfrogging HBL, UBL, Meezan, and all local FinTechs across all five dimensions simultaneously.

Brand Strategy Consulting

Bata Re-Imagined: Vision 2030

Following a 52% YoY profit drop in CY24 and Bata Pakistan slipping to 2nd in market share and 4th in brand value, this deck proposes a full brand rejuvenation across four strategic pillars.

The Problem

Bata carries six parity brands (Bubble Gummers, Red Label, Northstar, Power, Comfit, Weinbrenner) but suffers from aged brand perception and being seen as "ordinary" by increasingly brand-conscious Gen Z consumers.

The RMB Roadmap

Retail Transformation: Revamp 50 flagship stores with clear in-store brand segregation. Visual merchandising aligned to youth fashion trends.

Reframing Global Identity: Localized yet globally aligned communication. Audit → Reposition → Product launch → Storytelling → Campaign.

Marketing Innovation: Gen Z campaigns, TikTok/Instagram/YouTube influencer partnerships, exclusive drops and campus activations, AR challenges and UGC.

Brand Repositioning: Mantra: "Affordable, Trendy, Global Footwear for the Modern Pakistan."

Financial Projections

Revenue 2030: PKR 20,784M (Strategy) vs. PKR 18,201M (BAU)  ·  Profit 2030: PKR 1,551M vs. PKR 935M  ·  Strategy outperforms meaningfully from 2026 onward.
